üìö Understanding GNN for Financial Default Prediction

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) represent a paradigm shift in financial risk assessment. By modeling companies and their relationships as a graph, GNNs can capture complex dependencies that traditional models miss.

üîÆ What is a GNN?

Graph Neural Networks are designed to work with graph-structured data. They can capture:

  • Structural relationships between entities
  • Information propagation through connections
  • Complex patterns (motifs) in network topology

üí° Why Use GNNs?

Traditional credit scoring considers companies in isolation. But in reality:

  • Companies are connected through loans & supply chains
  • Risk spreads through the network
  • Motif patterns indicate hidden risks

üî∫ Motif Patterns

Recurring subgraph structures with special meaning:

  • Triangles: Circular dependencies, mutual risk
  • Stars: Concentrated exposure
  • Chains: Sequential risk propagation

üéì Key Takeaways

Network Structure Matters

Companies connected to risky entities become risky themselves

Motifs Reveal Hidden Risk

Triangular and star patterns often indicate problematic structures

GNNs Combine Everything

Node features + network structure = better predictions
